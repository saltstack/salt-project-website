Nicholas Aronne and Michael Poore recently posted a fascinating blog entry on the VMware Tanzu blog entitled [Elevating Enterprise Compliance: Bitnami's Game-Changing Salt Integration Unveiled](https://tanzu.vmware.com/content/blog/elevating-enterprise-compliance-bitnami-s-game-changing-salt-integration-unveiled). It contains some news that members of the Salt Project community might find interesting.

The blog entry announces that Bitnami embedded the Salt Minion into Bitnami images available in the Bitnami Application Catalog:

> Quietly, and without too much fanfare until now, Bitnami have embedded the Salt Minion into every single OVA and cloud image in the Bitnami Application Catalog. It's deactivated by default so it has to be enabled to be used and should be pointed at a Salt Master, but you don't have to install it yourself! Instructions for how to complete these simple activation steps can be found in the Bitnami documentation. ...
>
> Now you can more easily use Salt to manage images deployed from the Bitnami Application Catalog alongside any other workloads and devices in your IT estate, all with open-source software (OSS).

To learn more, continue reading [Elevating Enterprise Compliance: Bitnami's Game-Changing Salt Integration Unveiled](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/research/open-source-maintainers).
